Sample Interview Questions

Q1
How do you assess a student's reading level and identify specific deficits?
Tip: Show knowledge of diagnostic tools (DIBELS, Fountas & Pinnell, running records), phonics assessments, and using data to pinpoint needs.
Q2
Describe your approach to evidence-based reading instruction.
Tip: Discuss the science of reading, structured literacy, explicit phonics instruction, vocabulary development, and comprehension strategies.
Q3
How do you model effective reading instruction for classroom teachers?
Tip: Show demonstration lessons, co-teaching, providing feedback, and building teacher capacity without creating defensiveness.
Q4
What is your experience with intervention programs for struggling readers?
Tip: Discuss specific programs (Orton-Gillingham, Wilson, Leveled Literacy Intervention), progress monitoring, and fidelity of implementation.
