Sample Interview Questions

Q1
How do you differentiate instruction for students with varying abilities?
Tip: Discuss specific strategies: tiered assignments, flexible grouping, choice boards, scaffolding, and how you assess readiness.
Q2
Describe your classroom management philosophy.
Tip: Show proactive approach: clear expectations, positive reinforcement, relationship building, and restorative practices over punitive measures.
Q3
How do you engage students who are disinterested or struggling?
Tip: Discuss relevance, real-world connections, building relationships, varied instructional methods, and finding what motivates each student.
Q4
How do you assess student learning and use data to inform instruction?
Tip: Discuss formative and summative assessments, data analysis, reteaching strategies, and how you communicate progress to students and parents.
