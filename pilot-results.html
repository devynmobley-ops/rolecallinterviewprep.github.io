Findings

Key Takeaways

🚀

Trades students were the most engaged

Welding and electrical students averaged 8.2 sessions — higher than any other program. These students often have fewer interview prep resources available, and RoleCall filled a real gap.

📝

Resume Tailor drove re-engagement

Students who used Resume Tailor were 2.4x more likely to return for a second mock interview session. The resume feature acted as a "hook" that pulled students deeper into the platform.

🎯

Students explored beyond their program

34% of students practiced for at least one career outside their declared major. This suggests RoleCall is useful for career exploration, not just interview prep for a known path.

💪

Confidence increased significantly

In post-pilot surveys, 78% of participating students reported feeling "more confident" about interviewing, and 62% said they felt prepared for interviews in their field — up from 31% pre-pilot.

Recommendations

What We'd Do Differently

  • Integrate into the syllabus. The highest-engagement cohorts were those where instructors made RoleCall part of an assignment, not just an optional resource. Even a simple "complete 2 mock interviews" requirement dramatically increased adoption.
  • Promote Resume Tailor early. Students who discovered Resume Tailor on their own were more engaged. Making it part of the first-week assignment would boost retention.
  • Run a mid-pilot check-in. A 10-minute in-class demo at the halfway point reminded students the tool existed and drove a spike in usage during weeks 3-4.
  • Target specific programs. The broadest adoption came from programs where the instructor actively promoted RoleCall. Reaching out to program leads before the pilot starts increases buy-in.
