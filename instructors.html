Integrating RoleCall Into Your Course

This guide provides ready-to-use assignment templates, rubrics, and lesson plans for integrating RoleCall into Career Readiness, Professional Development, First-Year Experience, Capstone, and Co-op/Internship courses.

Every assignment below can be dropped directly into your syllabus. They're designed to work with RoleCall's existing features — no custom integration or IT support required.

Getting Started: If your school has a promo code, share it with students at the start of the semester. Students visit rollcallinterviewprep.com, enter the code in the More tab, and get instant Pro access. If you need a promo code, email rollcallinterviewprep@outlook.com.
Assignment 1

Career Exploration & Research

Best for: First-Year Experience, Career Exploration, undecided students
Time: 1-2 weeks • Points: 50-100

🔍
Explore 5 Career Paths
Research + Reflection Paper

Instructions for Students

  1. Go to rollcallinterviewprep.com and browse the career categories (Tech, Healthcare, Business, Trades, etc.)
  2. Choose 5 roles that interest you — at least 2 from different industries
  3. For each role, review: the job description, salary range, required education, certifications, and typical coursework
  4. Complete one mock interview for your top choice role
  5. Write a 2-page reflection covering:
    • Which roles surprised you and why
    • What education/certifications each path requires
    • How your current skills and coursework align with your top choice
    • What you learned from the mock interview experience

Deliverable

Submit your reflection paper along with a screenshot of your RoleCall practice history (found in the More tab → History) showing the mock interview you completed.

Assignment 2

Interview Preparation Portfolio

Best for: Career Readiness, Professional Development, Co-op Prep
Time: 3-4 weeks • Points: 100-150

💼
Build Your Interview Toolkit
Multi-part portfolio project

Part 1: Target Role Analysis (Week 1)

  • Identify your target role on RoleCall
  • Review all 12 interview questions and expert tips
  • Document the required education, certifications, and career path
  • Identify 3 skills you already have and 3 you need to develop

Part 2: Mock Interview Practice (Weeks 2-3)

  • Complete 3 full mock interviews for your target role
  • After each session, review your score and identify areas for improvement
  • Practice answering questions out loud (RoleCall is designed for verbal practice)
  • Track your improvement across all 3 sessions

Part 3: Resume Tailoring (Week 3)

  • Find a real job posting for your target role
  • Use RoleCall's Resume Tailor to analyze your resume against the job description
  • Implement the suggested changes
  • Save both the "before" and "after" versions

Part 4: Final Reflection (Week 4)

  • Write a 3-page reflection covering your journey from Week 1 to now
  • Include your score progression across the 3 mock interviews
  • Discuss what you learned about yourself and your career readiness
  • Outline a 6-month plan for closing your remaining skill gaps

Deliverable

Submit a portfolio PDF containing: target role analysis, practice history screenshots (all 3 sessions), before/after resume, and final reflection.

Assignment 3

Mock Interview Lab (In-Class)

Best for: Any career course, workshop, or orientation session
Time: One class period (50-75 min) • Points: 25-50 (participation)

🏆
Live Mock Interview Session
In-class activity + group discussion

Setup (5 minutes)

  • Students open rollcallinterviewprep.com on their phones or laptops
  • Each student navigates to their target role (or a role you assign)
  • If you have a promo code, students redeem it now

Practice Round 1 (10 minutes)

  • Every student starts a 3-question mock interview for their role
  • Students answer each question out loud (whispered or in their head if in a shared space)
  • After each question, they review the expert tip

Partner Practice (15 minutes)

  • Pair students up. Partner A asks Partner B one question from their RoleCall session
  • Partner B answers out loud while Partner A listens
  • Partner A gives feedback based on the expert tip they see on screen
  • Switch roles and repeat

Group Discussion (15 minutes)

  • Ask the class: "What was the hardest question you encountered?"
  • Discuss: "What did the expert tips tell you that you wouldn't have known?"
  • Share: "What's one thing you'll do differently in your next real interview?"

Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

  • Students screenshot their practice history as proof of completion
  • Assign homework: Complete 2 more mock interviews before next class
Instructor Tip: This works especially well as a mid-semester check-in or the week before career fairs. Students are often surprised by how different it feels to answer out loud vs. in their head.
Assignment 4

Job Offer Negotiation Simulation

Best for: Advanced career courses, MBA programs, senior capstone
Time: 2 weeks • Points: 100

💰
Negotiate Like a Pro
Research + Role-play + Reflection

Part 1: Salary Research (Week 1)

  • Using RoleCall's career data (salary ranges shown on each role), research the market rate for your target role in your geographic area
  • Use 2 additional sources (Glassdoor, BLS, Payscale) to cross-reference
  • Document your findings: low, mid, and high salary ranges

Part 2: Negotiation Script (Week 1-2)

  • Review the Job Offer Kit (available on RoleCall) for salary negotiation scripts
  • Write a custom negotiation script for a hypothetical offer at the mid-range salary
  • Include: how you'd counter, what benefits you'd negotiate, and how you'd handle a "no"

Part 3: Role-Play (Week 2)

  • In pairs, one student plays the hiring manager, the other the candidate
  • The "hiring manager" offers a salary at the low end of the range
  • The candidate uses their script to negotiate
  • Switch roles and repeat with a different scenario

Deliverable

Submit: salary research document, negotiation script, and a 1-page reflection on what you learned about negotiation and what you'd do differently in a real scenario.
